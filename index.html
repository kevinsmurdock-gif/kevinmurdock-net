The Citadel, Class of 1992

Kevin graduated from The Citadel, The Military College of South Carolina, in the Class of 1992 — earning Gold Star recognition for academic achievement, playing walk-on soccer, and completing his degree early.

Before the Citadel: Shannon Forest Christian School in Greenville, SC. After: a World Bank forestry project in Tunisia (1996–97), then a return to the Upstate to start what would become a 25-year career building healthcare companies.

The Citadel doesn't just teach leadership. It teaches showing up. That became the operating principle for every venture that followed.

The Sales Chapter

AstraZeneca · 2001–2004

Before founding companies, Kevin built one of the top national pharmaceutical sales territories at AstraZeneca — launching two blockbuster drugs into the South Carolina market.

Nexium Launch

Led the South Carolina market launch of Nexium, a proton-pump inhibitor that became one of AstraZeneca's most successful products. Top territory, top rep.

Crestor Launch

Launched Crestor across the SC market — one of the most important statin launches in recent pharmaceutical history. The groundwork for a career understanding how drugs reach providers.

Exclusive US Provider of DiabetesPredict
Premier Medical becomes the only US laboratory offering the DiabetesPredict diagnostic test — a genetic test for diabetes risk.

March 24, 2020
First FDA-Validated COVID Lab in South Carolina
On March 24, 2020, Premier Medical became the first private commercial laboratory in South Carolina validated for COVID-19 testing under FDA Emergency Use Authorization. The state was fewer than three weeks into the pandemic. We were first.

Spring 2020
Free COVID Testing for SC First Responders + N95 Donations
Free COVID-19 tests for every law enforcement officer, firefighter, and EMT/paramedic in South Carolina. Thousands of N95 masks donated to SC police, fire departments, and hospital systems.
2020
Yale SalivaDirect Designated Laboratory
Premier Medical named as a Yale SalivaDirect designated testing laboratory — one of a small number of US labs authorized to run Yale's breakthrough open-source COVID-19 saliva testing protocol.
